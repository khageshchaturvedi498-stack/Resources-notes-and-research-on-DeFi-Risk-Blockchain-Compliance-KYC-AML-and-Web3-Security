# Trade-Based Money Laundering — Key Notes
Source: UNODC eLearning Certification | Completed: March 2026

## What is TBML?
Trade-Based Money Laundering (TBML) is the process of disguising proceeds of crime through trade transactions to legitimise their illicit origins.

## The 6 Core TBML Techniques
1. **Over-Invoicing** — Goods invoiced above market value. Value moves Importer → Exporter
2. **Under-Invoicing** — Goods invoiced below market value. Requires collusion of both parties
3. **Multiple Invoicing** — Same shipment invoiced more than once
4. **Phantom Shipments** — Fictitious trade. No goods moved. Payment justifies value transfer
5. **False Description of Goods** — Misrepresentation of type, quality or quantity
6. **Short/Over-Shipping** — Quantity overstated or understated on documents

## Red Flags
- Invoice price significantly above or below market value
- Same trading parties transacting repeatedly
- Vague goods description
- Payment routed through unnecessary third countries
- Free trade zone involvement
- Stablecoin (USDT) settlements for trade transactions

## TBML in Crypto / Web3
- Over/under invoicing replicated through NFT sales at artificial prices
- Phantom transactions through DeFi protocols
- Value transfer through stablecoin settlements
- Cross-chain bridges used to obscure trail
- DEX swaps used to layer funds

## Money Laundering Typologies
| Typology | Method |
|---|---|
| TBML | Over/under invoicing, phantom shipments |
| Casino ML | Buying chips for cash, redeeming as cheque |
| Corporate Vehicles | Shell companies in business arrangements |
| Real Estate | Purchasing property from crime proceeds |
| Public Procurement | Slush funds in procurement agreements |

## Terror Financing vs Money Laundering
| | Money Laundering | Terror Financing |
|---|---|---|
| Source | Illegal | Legal OR Illegal |
| Purpose | Conceal dirty money | Fund terrorism |
| Detection | Moderate | High — clean funds harder to detect |

## Key Takeaway
TBML is hardest to detect because both trading parties collude, documents look legitimate, and no single party raises a red flag. Pattern recognition and subject matter expertise are essential.
